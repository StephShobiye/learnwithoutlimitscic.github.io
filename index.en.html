Parent-led support in Wales

Practical ALN support for families in Wales

Clear, parent-led guidance for families navigating ALN rights, IDPs, EOTAS, attendance pressure, school breakdown, home education and post-16 transitions.

Start with practical guidance, join the parent community, or explore our developing Bridge pathway for re-engagement and progression.

Wales-wide • Practical guidance • Community insight • Bilingual access

Supporting around 960 families across Wales since 2013, our work is shaped by recurring patterns shared by parents navigating Additional Learning Needs, education, and health systems.

Families across Wales are often navigating complex systems without clear pathways. We focus on making those pathways visible, usable, and less overwhelming.

No pressure to speak or participate in any particular way. You can take one step at a time.

Our work sits between lived experience and system design, helping families navigate while highlighting where systems break down in practice.

What we do

We provide practical, real-world support for families navigating the ALN system in Wales.

We run regular sessions, publish ongoing guidance, and build tools based on real questions families are asking across Wales.

Ways to connect

Choose the route that feels most useful right now. You can join the community, read practical guidance, come to an event, or explore our developing Bridge pathway.

Our open-call briefings bring together parent insight and system-level discussion, creating space for shared understanding across Wales.

Why Learn Without Limits exists

We do not replace statutory services. We help families navigate them more effectively, particularly at the points where support is delayed, unclear, or difficult to access.

This work is informed by the anonymised aggregated patterns we see repeatedly across families, not isolated cases, giving a clearer picture of how systems are actually working in practice.
