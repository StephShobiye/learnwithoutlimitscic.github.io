Parent Guide App Prototype

An early proof-of-concept digital tool testing whether community insight can become usable navigation for families in Wales.

View the prototype Send feedback

What the prototype was designed to test

This prototype was developed to answer one core methodology question:

Can our community insight loop actually produce usable digital navigation worthy of seeking funding to develop?

Our answer is yes.

The Parent Guide App is currently an early HTML proof-of-concept prototype. It is intentionally rough and is not yet a fully operational mobile app or finished public product.

Its purpose has been to help Learning Without Limits CIC and our community test whether our iterative development method works in this context. The prototype allows us to explore whether recurring parent questions, anonymised aggregate community patterns and lived-experience feedback can be translated into clearer digital navigation pathways for families.

This means the prototype is not the final technical product. It is a low-cost learning tool that has helped us test structure, user journeys, content organisation and the role a future mobile-first app could play within the Prevent layer of our wider Prevent → Bridge → Progress model.

Future development may include a mobile app and, if it can be governed safely, AI-enabled chatbot functionality. Any use of AI would need strong technical governance, content validation, safeguarding controls, accessibility testing and clear limits. The priority is not novelty; it is accurate, safe and accessible navigation for families.

How this fits within our wider model

Learn Without Limits CIC supports families through three connected stages:

  • Prevent - guidance, information, and tools to help families navigate earlier
  • Bridge - re-engagement and confidence-building when education begins to break down
  • Progress - return routes into school, college, training, or work through handover to statutory or mainstream systems

The App Prototype sits within the Prevent layer. Its role is to make information, navigation, and practical support easier to access earlier.

Prevent, Bridge and Progress

A simple view of how Learn Without Limits helps families earlier, supports re-engagement when education begins to break down, and then signposts progression through onward handover.

Progress itself is usually delivered by schools, colleges, training providers, employers, and statutory services. Learn Without Limits helps families engage earlier, more clearly, and more effectively before handover.

What the prototype is for

The prototype has helped us test whether community insight, lived-experience feedback and recurring parent questions can be turned into clearer digital routes through the ALN system in Wales.

  • testing structure and navigation
  • checking whether aggregate community patterns can become usable pathways
  • exploring how guidance can be organised more clearly
  • learning from lived experience before full development
  • showing funders and partners what a future mobile tool could become

What it is not yet

  • not yet a fully developed mobile app
  • not yet a finished public product
  • not yet a replacement for professional advice or statutory services
  • not yet an AI chatbot
  • not yet the final technical build we intend to scale

What the future app may include

  • clear guidance on Welsh ALN pathways
  • parent FAQs and practical navigation support
  • printable templates for letters, requests, and IDP-related processes
  • signposting to trusted resources and community support
  • wellbeing and reflection tools for families under pressure
  • carefully governed AI-enabled navigation support, only if it can be made safe and accurate

These features are still being shaped through iterative development, lived experience, and testing.

Why funding matters

The prototype demonstrates that the method is worth developing, but moving from rough proof-of-concept to a properly operational mobile-first product will require technical leadership, development capacity, safeguarding-by-design, accessibility testing, content validation, version control, and structured delivery.

Our aim is to secure funding for a technical governance lead who can oversee a safe move to a mobile stack, manage technical risk, support appropriate development capacity, and ensure that future product decisions are led by user need rather than technology for its own sake.

This would allow us to build a more robust, accessible, and sustainable digital tool for families across Wales.
